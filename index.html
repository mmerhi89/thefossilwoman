A tribute to Mary Anning

The Fossil Woman

Mary Anning, a collector of fossils walks along the Jurassic coast. The coast is her refuge broken with traces of impermanent souls echoing shattered patterns of the consciousness. The more she collects, the more she craves to satisfy her internal hunger. The fossil woman seeks to revive her sense of soul and self when embarking into the unknown. It is through the strangeness, she feels a sensation of familiarity. A calling from the deep depths of the water. She hears murmurs coming from the underworld, ones that no longer bear to be deafened, but yearn to be heard. And it is in this solitude her resonant thoughts are surfaced from the water confronting the unconscious. In her ability to enter this transition, the fossil woman is no longer parched, but wholly.

Findings

Sea lilies are predators of the underworld, and serve as a memorial for those who depart and arrive. They are the keeper of cycles and eyes of fleeting souls.
